Colloquial language in Cooper Clarke's poems


In many of Cooper Clarke's poems, the speaker directly addresses the reader, as if they are in a conversation. The words used to denote this include pronouns, such as "I" or "you", but also "we" and the use of the impertaive form, as if the speaker were giving the reader instructions or advice.
